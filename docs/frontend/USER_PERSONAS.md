# SpeakLift: User Personas

To design an empathetic and effective interface, we must understand the humans using SpeakLift. The platform serves young, ambitious individuals navigating one of the most high-stakes transitions of their lives.

## Persona 1: "The Anxious Senior"
**Profile:**
- **Age**: 21
- **Role**: Final Year CS Undergrad
- **Goal**: Secure a Software Engineering placement before graduation.
- **Pain Points**: Suffers from imposter syndrome. Has strong technical skills (can pass LeetCode) but freezes up during behavioral or system design verbal explanations.

**How SpeakLift Helps:**
- **The Design**: The UI must be exceptionally calming. Harsh reds for mistakes will only increase anxiety. Feedback must be constructive and framed as "Here's how to sound even better" rather than "You failed this."
- **The Feature**: The *Readiness Score* gives them objective, mathematical proof that they are prepared, combating their subjective anxiety.

## Persona 2: "The Career Switcher / Bootcamper"
**Profile:**
- **Age**: 26
- **Role**: Junior Developer / Recent Bootcamp Grad
- **Goal**: Break into their first tech role after transitioning from a non-technical background.
- **Pain Points**: Lacks the vocabulary and "industry jargon" that traditional CS grads use naturally. Unsure how to frame their past non-tech experience as an asset.

**How SpeakLift Helps:**
- **The Design**: The *AI Insight* component is crucial here. The UI needs to heavily spotlight the Vocabulary Statistics and Concept Coverage metrics to show them exactly which industry terms they are missing.
- **The Feature**: The AI Mentor tone should be deeply educational, highlighting exactly how to rephrase their answers to match industry expectations.

## Persona 3: "The High Achiever"
**Profile:**
- **Age**: 22
- **Role**: Top-tier University Grad
- **Goal**: Secure a role at a FAANG / Tier-1 tech company.
- **Pain Points**: Already good at interviewing but needs to be *flawless*. Looking for micro-optimizations in their delivery, confidence, and semantic relevance to specific Job Descriptions.

**How SpeakLift Helps:**
- **The Design**: Needs high data density. They want to see the exact cosine similarity scores, the Flesch-Kincaid readability grade, and a breakdown of their filler words. The UI must accommodate deep dives into the analytics without feeling cluttered.
- **The Feature**: *Interview Timeline* and *Skill Progress* charts. They are motivated by streaks, grinding, and watching the chart go up to the 99th percentile.

## Shared Characteristics
Across all personas, these users are:
- **Digital Natives**: They immediately recognize clunky, outdated software. If it looks like their university's legacy portal, they will not trust the AI's advice.
- **Time-Poor**: They are juggling classes, part-time jobs, and hundreds of applications. The UI must respect their time. Quick actions and summarized executive reports are vital.
- **Mobile-Reliant**: They will often read their interview reports on their phones while commuting or lying in bed. Mobile responsiveness is non-negotiable.
